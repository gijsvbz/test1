## About the Cheetah

The cheetah (*Acinonyx jubatus*) is a large wild cat known for being the fastest land animal.
It is native primarily to parts of Africa and a small region in Iran. Cheetahs are built for speed, with a lightweight body, long legs, and a flexible spine that allows them to cover large distances quickly while sprinting.

---

## Speed and Hunting

Cheetahs are capable of reaching speeds of up to around 100–120 km/h (62–75 mph) in short bursts.
Unlike many other big cats, they rely heavily on sight rather than smell to locate prey. Their hunts usually occur during the day, where they stalk animals such as gazelles and then chase them in rapid sprints.

Because these high-speed chases require a lot of energy, they typically last less than a minute.

---

## Physical Characteristics

Cheetahs have several distinctive features:

* A slender, aerodynamic body
* Long legs and a deep chest
* A long tail used for balance while turning at high speeds
* Black “tear marks” running from the eyes down the sides of the muzzle

Their spotted coats provide camouflage in grassy savannas and open plains.

---

## Habitat and Conservation

Cheetahs prefer open landscapes such as grasslands, savannas, and semi-desert regions.
Although they were once widespread across Africa and parts of Asia, their population has declined due to habitat loss, human conflict, and reduced prey availability.

Today, conservation programs aim to protect cheetah populations and maintain the ecosystems where they live.
